## Why Local Search Matters for Your Business

When someone in Cincinnati or Columbus types "wedding coordinator near me" or "catering for corporate event Columbus" into Google, the very first thing they see is a map with three highlighted businesses — called the **local pack**. Below that are the regular website results.

Getting into that local pack is one of the most valuable things you can do for your business. The businesses listed there get the majority of clicks, calls, and inquiries from that search. It is essentially a free, permanent advertisement that targets people who are actively looking for exactly what you offer.

The good news is that local pack ranking is largely within your control. It is based on three things:

1. **Relevance** — Does Google understand what services you offer?
2. **Distance** — How far is the searcher from your location?
3. **Prominence** — How trusted and well-known does Google believe your business to be?

The actions below directly build relevance and prominence. Distance is geographic, but your service area extending 100 miles means you can appear for searches across a wide region.

## Action 1: Google Business Profile

### What it is
Google Business Profile (formerly called Google My Business) is a free listing managed directly through Google. It is the source of everything that appears when someone searches for your business by name — the map pin, your hours, photos, reviews, services, and the "Call" or "Get directions" buttons. It is also what determines whether you appear in the local pack for general searches like "event caterer Cincinnati."

### Why it matters
This is the single most important action on this list. Your website tells Google about your business, but your Google Business Profile *is* your business in Google's eyes. Without a complete, verified profile, you are effectively invisible in Google Maps and the local pack — regardless of how well your website is optimized.

Think of it this way: your website is your storefront, and your Google Business Profile is the sign on the street that tells people you exist.

### What to do

1. **Go to** [business.google.com](https://business.google.com)
2. **Search for your business name.** If a profile already exists (sometimes Google auto-creates one), claim it. If not, create a new one.
3. **Verify your business.** Google will send a postcard, phone call, or email to confirm you are the real owner. Complete this step — unverified profiles do not appear in search results.
4. **Fill out every section completely:**
   - Business name: **Gather Catering and Events** *(must match your website exactly — see Action 2)*
   - Category: Select **Caterer** as your primary category. Add secondary categories such as **Wedding Planner**, **Event Planner**, and **Bartending Service**
   - Service area: Add Cincinnati, OH and Columbus, OH, and any surrounding areas you serve within your 100-mile radius
   - Hours: Add your general availability or contact hours
   - Phone number: Add your business phone *(see Action 2 note)*
   - Website: `https://gathercateringandevents.com`
   - Description: Write 2–3 sentences describing Gather's services and markets served. Use natural language — do not stuff keywords.
   - Services: Add each of your six services individually (Full Planning, Day-Of Coordinating, Mobile Bartending, Catering, Catering Staffing, Mobile Food Cart)
   - Photos: Add at least 10 high-quality photos (see photo notes below)

5. **Add photos.** Businesses with photos receive significantly more clicks and direction requests than those without. Aim for:
   - A cover photo (a strong event or food image)
   - Your logo
   - 5–10 event photos showing your work across different service types

## Action 2: NAP Consistency

### What it is
NAP stands for **Name, Address, and Phone number**. NAP consistency means that your business name, address, and phone number are written *identically* everywhere they appear online — your website, Google, The Knot, WeddingWire, Yelp, Facebook, and any other directory.

### Why it matters
Google cross-references your business information across the internet to confirm that your business is real and legitimate. When the same name, address, and phone number appear consistently across many sources, Google's confidence in your business increases — and your ranking improves.

Inconsistencies — even small ones like "Gather Catering & Events" in one place and "Gather Catering and Events" in another, or a different phone number on Yelp vs. Google — create conflicting signals that reduce Google's confidence and hurt your ranking.

### What to do

1. **Decide on your exact business name** and use it everywhere, character for character:
   > **Gather Catering and Events**

2. **Decide on a single phone number** to use as your primary business contact. This should be the number that will appear on your website, Google Business Profile, The Knot, WeddingWire, and everywhere else.

3. **Audit your existing listings.** Search Google for "Gather Catering and Events" and check every result — your website, social profiles, directory listings. Anywhere your business name, address, or phone appears, verify it matches exactly.

4. **Update any mismatches** you find on each platform.

> **Note:** Your website currently does not display a phone number. Once a primary business number is confirmed, it should be added to the website footer so it matches your other listings. Please share your preferred business phone number with the web team.

## Action 5: Review Generation

### What it is
Review generation is an ongoing practice of asking satisfied clients to leave a review on Google after their event.

### Why it matters
Reviews are one of the most direct ranking factors for the Google local pack. Google interprets a steady stream of positive reviews as evidence that a business is active, legitimate, and delivers on its promises. Businesses with more reviews — especially recent ones — rank higher and get clicked more.

Beyond ranking, reviews are the first thing most people look at when evaluating a vendor. A page of genuine, specific reviews from happy clients is one of the most powerful conversion tools available.

### What to do

1. **Create a direct Google review link** for your business:
   - Go to your Google Business Profile
   - Find the section labeled "Get more reviews" or "Share review form"
   - Copy the short link it generates (something like `g.page/[yourbusiness]/review`)

2. **Send the link after each event.** Within a few days of a successful event, send a short, personal follow-up message to your client. Something simple works best:

   > *"It was such a pleasure working with you on [event]. If you have a moment, we'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps other couples and clients find us. Here's a direct link: [link]. Thank you so much!"*

3. **Do this consistently** — even two or three new reviews per month compounds quickly and builds a meaningful advantage over competitors who don't ask.

4. **Respond to every review**, positive or negative. Thank reviewers by name, reference a specific detail from their event if you can, and address any concerns professionally. Google rewards businesses that engage with their reviews.

> **Note:** Do not offer discounts or incentives in exchange for reviews — this violates Google's policies and can result in reviews being removed or your profile being penalized.

## Action 6: Local Citations

### What it is
A local citation is any mention of your business name, address, and phone number on a website other than your own. Directories, chamber of commerce listings, event industry guides, and local business listings are all citations.

### Why it matters
Citations are a trust signal. The more places Google sees consistent information about your business, the more confident it becomes that you are a legitimate, established local business — and the higher it ranks you. Think of it as a vote of confidence from each directory that lists you.

### What to do

Below are the most valuable citation sources for a catering and events business in Cincinnati and Columbus. Submit or claim your listing on each one, and ensure your NAP is identical across all of them.

**General business directories:**
- **Yelp** — [biz.yelp.com](https://biz.yelp.com) — Claim your listing and add photos and services
- **Facebook Business Page** — Create or update your business page with full contact info and services
- **Better Business Bureau** — [bbb.org](https://www.bbb.org) — A BBB listing adds credibility

**Wedding and event industry directories:**
- **Zola** — [pros.zola.com](https://pros.zola.com) — Growing wedding marketplace
- **Thumbtack** — [thumbtack.com](https://www.thumbtack.com) — Popular for local event services
- **GigSalad** — [gigsalad.com](https://www.gigsalad.com) — Event vendor marketplace

**Local directories:**
- **Cincinnati Chamber of Commerce** — Contact the local chamber to submit a business listing
- **Columbus Chamber of Commerce** — Same for the Columbus market
- **Local bridal show directories** — Any bridal expos or shows you participate in will often have an online vendor directory; ensure your listing is current
